Q1 What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?

The .git directory is created to keep track of repository metadata

Q2 How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository? What value do you think it offers?

The staging area is midway between working and repository - think purgatory
between earth and heaven.

Q3 How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical change?

By only adding the files that contribute to that logical change.

Q4 What are some situations when branches would be helpful in keeping your history organized? How would branches help?

trying out a new feature, keeping development and stable branches apart.

Q5 How do the diagrams help you visualize the branch structure?

The branches enable visualisation of different areas of development
and the commits that they consist of.

Q6 What is the result of merging two branches together? Why do we represent it in the diagram the way we do?

Changes from two branches are combined to establish a new master with 
the code from the other branch included.
